ON TAP

DE DAY DA

AMERICAN CREAM ALE

Because we’re easy come, easy go. This is Dad’s fridge beer, a tow the line Passaico style Yacht Rock Cream Ale crossed with a Mexican Lager. Get back in the high life, lido shuffle on in to the parti', karamu', fiesta, ssudio, forever...no jacket required, its’ kiss should be on your list. Our Cream Ale is made for times like these, when it’s hotter than two squirrels in a wool sock. We’re just giving you the beat boys, ya’ll gotta listen to the music. Light, crisp, clean, little sweet...no gimmicks concession style fizz. Flavors of steamed corn, white grain breads, orchard esters, with balanced bitterness.

ABV: 4.7%

CAN'T OR WON'T?

WHITE IPA

Would you say we’d be venturing into a…zone of danger? This meadow of graffiti is a collision of intention. The incrustation is pale, rye, wheat, oats and spelt. And the patina a rhythmically curious blend of Denali, Southern Cross, Rakau hops & blended belgian yeasts. Hawaiian Punch guy with a fistful of countryside. Flavors of citrus, grass and fruit punch.

ABV: 6.2%

HOPS: Denali, Southern Cross, & Rakau

HARDOG

AMERICAN IPA

Harlee. Hardiggity. HD. HLeeD. Hardo. Hardoglty. Here we have sweeter IPA with a helping of El Dorado, Simcoe and CTZ hops all in honor of a great pup. A persistently jovial and gentle rescue dog who always took her time to smell the roses, grass, leaves, and anything else on the block - take the time to enjoy Hardog.

ABV: 6.9%

OG: 1.065

HOPS: CTZ, Simcoe & El Dorado

YEAST: English

HERE THERE BE MONSTERS

AMERICAN IPA

Ye’ intruders beware, we’re off the edges of the map. Here...there be monsters. So here’s to the Great Meadows lizard man, the Matawan man-eater, the Cape May sea serpent and the NJ devil. This hoppy, swaggy, swill is made for yer’ grogs! IPA sowed full of flaked oats, blend of pale and pilsner oats, and a thunderous clap of Vic Secret, Rakau, and Wakatu hops. Notes of rich and sweet fragrant pineapple, grassy pine meadows, peaches & muskmelon.

ABV: 6.8%

OG: 1.068

MALT: Pilsner, Pale, Oats, Wheat, Munich, Dextrose

HOPS: Wakatu, Rakau, Vic Secret & Motueka

YEAST: English

LA DI DA DI

MILK PORTER

For all ya’ll keeping ya’ll in health just to see you smile and enjoy yourself. Best believe we gonna’ show you how to do it. We wound back and hammered our cozy milk porter with toasted coconut, Madagascar vanilla beans and bourbon barrel aged coffee beans from Homestead roasters. Emulsified wave of deliciousness. Notes of bourbon, almond joy, macadamia, subtle roasty crust, fudgy cocoa and vanilla frosting.

ABV: 6.4%

OG: 1.066

MALT: Pale, Munich, Brown, Chocolate, Crystal, Oats, Flaked Barley, Carafa

HOPS: Warrior

YEAST: English

MISC: Toasted Coconut, Vanilla Beans, Bourbon Barrel Aged Coffee Beans

* Contains Lactose

COLLECTING MOONBEAMS

MILK STOUT

Curvy sticks and wooden poles assistin’ you in plugging holes, plug them holes till you see straight through to the mind's eye. We’re going dark side with the cherry six four. The tectonic heels of this dark fudgy ink are all bad-ass American Stout, classic constructed, sweet malt & bitter roasted chocolate. Inside the thin candy shell, a spongy eye of cherry puree; just enough to initiate the sequence and cause a mutation in the roasty rift. Notes of coffee rinds, cocoa and chocolate dipped cherry.

ABV: 5.8%

MISC: Tart Cherry

Freshness & Variety Enjoyed Locally

Twin Elephant Brewing Company's vision is to offer an extremely fresh variety of high quality, handcrafted ales and lagers brewed mindfully with locally sourced ingredients when available. Our portfolio of beers are not restricted in style or flavor but will have a decided focus on all the classic styles that we have enjoyed drinking for a decade and a half. Our vision is for our patrons to enjoy these unique local beers in a relaxing and enjoyable atmosphere that augments their tasting experience and to share their thoughts with each other as well as us so we can continually improve.

Twin Elephant Brewing Company is founded by three partners with the dream of turning their passion for brewing craft beers into an exciting shared adventure with the local community. Scott McLuskey & Tim Besecker have been homebrewing together for over 10 years starting, like most folks do, in the garage and for the last 5 years Cindy DeRama has joined the crew with a proclivity for recipe formulation using unique ingredients. Throughout the years we have honed our craft, learning and practicing the artistry and science of brewing. All three of us have entrepreneurial experience through both education and acting as key cogs in several small businesses throughout the years.
